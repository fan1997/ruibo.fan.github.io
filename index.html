About

I am a 3rd-year Ph.D. candidate at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou) advised by Professor Xiaowen Chu and co-advised by Professor Wei Wang (HKUST CSE Department). My research interests lie in high-performance computing, particularly focused on GPU architecture and optimization. I am currently investigating methods to accelerate sparse matrix operations and graph neural networks on modern GPU architectures.

Prior to joining HKUST(GZ), I received my Master's degree from Peking University (2019-2022) at the Academy for Advanced Interdisciplinary Studies, and my Bachelor's degree from Huazhong University of Science and Technology (2015-2019) in the School of Artificial Intelligence and Automation.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical computer architecture and practical performance optimization for real-world applications. My goal is to develop efficient algorithms and systems that can fully utilize modern hardware capabilities to accelerate computational workloads across various domains including AI, scientific computing, and large-scale data processing.

Research Projects

Efficient Large Language Model Inference with Sparsity

2024 - Present

Developing SpInfer, a system that leverages low-level sparsity patterns in LLM computations to accelerate inference on GPUs. Our approach identifies and exploits sparsity opportunities at different levels of the inference pipeline, achieving significant speedups over state-of-the-art inference engines.

Optimizing Sparse Matrix Operations with Tensor Cores

2023 - 2024

Developed novel techniques to leverage GPU tensor cores for accelerating sparse matrix multiplication operations, with applications in deep learning frameworks and scientific computing. Our DTC-SpMM approach significantly outperforms state-of-the-art implementations across various sparsity patterns.

Comprehensive Analysis of NVIDIA Hopper Architecture

2022 - 2024

Conducted in-depth analysis and microbenchmarking of NVIDIA's Hopper GPU architecture to understand performance characteristics, memory hierarchy, and execution behavior. This work provides insights for both system designers and application developers on effectively utilizing this latest architecture.

Accelerating Graph Neural Networks with Optimized GPU Kernels

2021 - 2023

Designed specialized GPU kernels for sparse operations in graph neural networks, focusing on the performance bottlenecks in message passing and aggregation phases. Our approach achieves up to 2.7× speedup over existing frameworks for GNN training.
